ATL (Atal Tinkering Lab)

At the age of 11, I was privileged to join ATL (Atal Tinkering Lab), an Indian government initiative aimed at nurturing a love for STEM in select students. ATL opened doors to diverse opportunities, including creating my own video game using Unity—a course facilitated by Microsoft in collaboration with ATL. This experience not only imparted technical knowledge in game development but also instilled in me a sense of purpose by educating me about UN sustainability goals and how STEM could be leveraged to achieve them. Worked on many projects related to UN sustainable goals and environment problems.

ATL continued to broaden my horizons with courses in Python programming and robotics. In a competition organized by ATL, challenging us to address real-life societal problems, I designed a device to aid individuals with hearing impairment in safely crossing roads—a cochlear implant equipped with a PIR sensor to detect approaching vehicles. These experiences further fueled my determination to contribute meaningfully to the evolving landscape of STEM and its potential to make a positive impact on society. .
